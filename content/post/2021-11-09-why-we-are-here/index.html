Why are we here? Well, why am I here? That’s a pretty important question, and to avoid the “food blog syndrome” with 700 words preceding a 48-word recipe, I think I’ll just start with the “how”.

I really love listening to podcasts. Driving, doing chores, exercising, I love it.

One day I heard the Planet Money episode titled Project REDMAP. In it, they outlined (and updated on a few years later) the out-in-the-open (and shockingly legal) Republican campaign to win over state legislative houses in order to control the redistricting process. That might not seem that bad, but imagine if a politician could take a surgical blade and cut you and your friends out of your district, and lump you in somewhere completely different. Think about the smartphone you had in 2010, and think about the phone you have now. That’s also how much advancement has been made in mapping software (all the while, the courts refused to rule on gerrymandering time and time again). This got me interested angry, so I started reading, first RatF-cked, then The Big Sort, and then found Dave’s Redistricting site and the Princeton Gerrymandering Project. And then finally I started googling and found a degree program at Georgetown: Data Science for Public Policy.

The only catch is that Gerrymandering is so easily fixable, it’s good to have a more broad focus of study. One tactic fixes the entire thing: Ranked Choice Voting. That could be installed with as little as the stroke of a pen, so it’s better to have a broader approach.
